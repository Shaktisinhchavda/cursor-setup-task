# Draft Playbook: YouTube Content Strategy for B2B SaaS

Date drafted: 2026-06-16

Status: draft based on collected metadata, public source notes, and company examples. This should be strengthened with actual transcripts and LinkedIn post evidence before being treated as final.

## Executive Summary

B2B SaaS YouTube strategy should not copy generic creator advice. The goal is not broad entertainment or viral reach. The goal is to create durable video assets that help the right buyers understand problems, evaluate approaches, trust the company, and move through the buying journey with less friction.

The research so far supports four operating principles:

1. Build around buyer problems, not channel vanity metrics.
2. Plan distribution before production.
3. Use repeatable content pillars instead of random video ideas.
4. Measure videos by job-to-be-done, not views alone.

## Recommended Channel Positioning

A B2B SaaS YouTube channel should be positioned as a practical education source for a specific buyer segment.

Good positioning:

- "We help RevOps leaders fix pipeline visibility problems."
- "We teach HubSpot teams how to build cleaner CRM workflows."
- "We help SaaS founders build repeatable go-to-market systems."

Weak positioning:

- "We post marketing tips."
- "We make videos about our product."
- "We want to grow on YouTube."

## Content Pillars

### 1. Search-Led Tutorials

These videos answer high-intent questions buyers and users already search for.

Best use cases:

- product-led SaaS
- workflow-heavy tools
- integration ecosystems
- onboarding and adoption
- evaluation-stage content

Examples from collected metadata:

- HubSpot Hacks: HubSpot workflow and segmentation tutorials
- Sam Dunning: SaaS SEO and AEO problem-solving videos

Recommendation:

Publish at least two search-led tutorials per month. Keep titles specific and outcome-led.

### 2. Category Point-of-View

These videos explain what is changing in the market and why buyers need a different approach.

Best use cases:

- high-consideration SaaS
- new categories
- founder-led content
- complex buying committees
- demand creation

Examples from collected metadata:

- Metadata.io: GTM, AI, and AEO topics
- TK Kader: SaaS growth and go-to-market strategy
- Cognism: outbound, RevOps, and sales signal topics

Recommendation:

Publish opinionated videos that name the old way, explain the shift, and give the buyer a better mental model.

### 3. Product-Led Education

These videos show the product inside a useful business workflow.

Best use cases:

- product evaluation
- demo follow-up
- customer onboarding
- feature adoption
- sales enablement

Examples from collected metadata:

- Wistia product and integration videos
- Vidyard sales/video workflow videos
- HubSpot Hacks workflow tutorials

Recommendation:

Do not make generic product tours. Show a role-specific workflow and the business result it supports.

### 4. Repurposable Expert Interviews

These videos create one source asset that can become many distribution assets.

Best use cases:

- lean content teams
- partner marketing
- customer education
- founder-led authority
- LinkedIn distribution

Examples from collected metadata:

- Cognism operator-led conversations
- Metadata.io GTM leader discussions
- Vidyard expert-led sales/video content

Recommendation:

Run interviews with a clear editorial angle. Each episode should produce a full video, clips, LinkedIn posts, newsletter copy, and sales snippets.

## Production Workflow

Each video should start with a short brief:

- Buyer role
- Pain point
- Search or demand signal
- Video pillar
- Key takeaway
- Call to action
- Distribution plan
- Success metric

Minimum team:

- channel owner
- subject-matter expert
- editor or producer
- distribution owner

Minimum cadence:

- one video per week if production is lightweight
- two videos per month if production requires heavier editing

## Distribution Workflow

Every video should become more than a YouTube upload.

Distribution checklist:

- YouTube upload with searchable title and description
- LinkedIn post from expert or founder
- 3 to 5 short clips
- email or newsletter section
- sales follow-up asset
- website/help/resource embed
- internal note for sales/customer success

This follows the Foundation source note: distribution should be planned as part of the content system, not added after publishing.

## Measurement Framework

Measure by content job:

| Content type | Main job | Useful metrics |
|---|---|---|
| Search-led tutorial | capture existing demand | search impressions, retention, product clicks, support usage |
| Category POV | create demand | comments, shares, saves, newsletter signups, sales mentions |
| Product-led education | support evaluation/adoption | demo-page engagement, onboarding usage, activation support |
| Expert interview | create source material | clips produced, partner shares, email clicks, sales usage |

Avoid judging all videos by view count. For B2B SaaS, qualified attention is more important than broad reach.

## First 90 Days

Month 1:

- 1 search-led tutorial
- 1 category POV
- 1 product-led education video
- 1 expert or internal SME interview

Month 2:

- repeat the formats that showed signal
- use sales/customer questions to generate new topics
- repurpose the strongest video into blog/email/social content

Month 3:

- formalize the best-performing format into a repeatable series
- create a 20-topic backlog
- define dashboard metrics by pillar
- review which videos sales and customer success reused

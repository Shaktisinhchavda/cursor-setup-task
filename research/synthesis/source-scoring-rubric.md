# Source Scoring Rubric

Date: 2026-06-16

Use this rubric to decide whether a video, LinkedIn post, article, podcast episode, or transcript is worth including in the final research corpus.

## Scoring

Score each source from 1 to 5 in each category.

| Category | 1 point | 3 points | 5 points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Practitioner credibility | Generic commentator | Some relevant experience | Active operator or specialist with direct B2B SaaS/video experience |
| Specificity | Broad advice | Some examples | Concrete tactics, workflows, metrics, or examples |
| B2B SaaS relevance | Generic creator advice | Partly transferable | Directly relevant to SaaS buyers, GTM, demand generation, or product education |
| YouTube/video relevance | Mostly unrelated | Mentions video/content | Directly informs YouTube, video, repurposing, or video-led distribution |
| Playbook usefulness | Interesting but hard to act on | Some useful ideas | Can become a clear recommendation, checklist, or framework |

## Include / Exclude Rules

Include sources that score:

- 18 or higher overall
- or 5 in playbook usefulness with strong B2B relevance

Exclude sources that:

- focus only on consumer creator growth
- optimize for vanity views without buyer relevance
- lack examples or operating detail
- are mostly promotional without useful strategy

## Notes For This Project

The evaluator cares more about 10 high-signal sources than a large pile of generic links. This rubric is intended to keep the corpus focused.

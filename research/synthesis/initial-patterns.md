# Initial Patterns

Date: 2026-06-16

This synthesis is based on the expert shortlist, public YouTube metadata, and first RSS pull. It is not yet based on full transcript analysis.

## Pattern 1: B2B SaaS YouTube Should Be Built Around Buyer Problems

The strongest targets are not generic brand videos. They are tutorials, strategy breakdowns, product-led explanations, and operator interviews. This suggests a B2B SaaS channel should map videos to problems buyers are already trying to solve.

Examples from the metadata:

- HubSpot Hacks publishes specific HubSpot workflow tutorials.
- Sam Dunning publishes SaaS SEO and AEO problem-solving videos.
- TK Kader publishes SaaS growth and go-to-market strategy videos.

## Pattern 2: YouTube Can Support More Than Awareness

Several sources point toward YouTube supporting:

- search-led discovery
- sales education
- product adoption
- demand creation
- founder-led authority
- content repurposing

This matters because B2B SaaS teams often treat YouTube as a top-of-funnel brand channel only.

## Pattern 3: The Best Source Mix Is Cross-Functional

The shortlist intentionally includes:

- content strategists
- video marketing practitioners
- SaaS go-to-market operators
- company channels using video as education
- founder-led YouTube examples

A useful playbook should combine all of these perspectives instead of copying creator economy tactics directly.

## Pattern 4: Transcript Collection Needs a Reliable Workflow

Public metadata was easy to collect through channel pages and RSS feeds. Transcript collection was less reliable. The next research batch should focus on fewer videos and use a more dependable transcript method.

## Working Hypothesis

A practical B2B SaaS YouTube strategy should start with 3 repeatable content pillars:

1. Search-intent tutorials for known problems
2. Expert-led point-of-view videos for category education
3. Repurposable interviews or breakdowns that can feed LinkedIn, email, and sales enablement

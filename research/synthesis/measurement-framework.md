# Measurement Framework

Date: 2026-06-16

A B2B SaaS YouTube program should be measured by the job each video performs. One metric cannot fairly evaluate every format.

## Channel-Level Metrics

- subscriber growth from target audience
- returning viewers
- qualified comments
- branded search lift
- referral traffic to product or resource pages
- assisted conversions
- sales team usage

## Video-Level Metrics By Content Type

| Content type | Primary job | Useful metrics |
|---|---|---|
| Search-led tutorial | Capture active problem demand | search impressions, click-through rate, average view duration, product page clicks |
| Product walkthrough | Explain workflow and reduce uncertainty | demo-page engagement, trial activation assistance, support deflection |
| Expert POV | Build trust and shape category beliefs | comments, saves, LinkedIn discussion, newsletter signups, sales mentions |
| Interview / podcast | Create source material for distribution | clips created, social engagement, partner shares, email clicks |
| Customer story | Build proof | sales usage, opportunity influence, watch completion by accounts |

## Early-Stage Metrics

For the first 90 days, focus on:

- publishing consistency
- topic-message fit
- retention curve quality
- qualitative feedback from buyers and sales
- repurposing output per video

## Later-Stage Metrics

After a content base exists, add:

- pipeline influence
- assisted conversion paths
- account-level engagement
- organic search visibility
- AI answer visibility and citations
- sales cycle support

## Measurement Principle

Views are useful, but they are not the strategy. For B2B SaaS, a video with 500 qualified views from the right buyer segment can be more valuable than a broad video with 50,000 irrelevant views.

# Playbook Outline: YouTube Content Strategy for B2B SaaS

Date: 2026-06-16

This is a working outline based on the expert shortlist, YouTube metadata, public source notes, and first synthesis. It should be refined after transcripts and LinkedIn posts are collected.

## 1. Strategy Foundation

Define the channel around a specific ICP, not a broad audience.

Core questions:

- Who is the buyer?
- What job are they trying to do?
- What questions do they research before speaking to sales?
- Which parts of the buying journey are hard to explain in text?
- Where will the video be distributed after YouTube?

## 2. Content Pillars

### Search-Led Tutorials

Purpose: capture active demand and support product education.

Examples:

- workflow walkthroughs
- integration tutorials
- "how to solve X" videos
- feature use cases
- category basics

Best for:

- product-led SaaS
- ecosystem tools
- complex workflows
- onboarding and adoption

### Expert Point-of-View Videos

Purpose: create demand and shape category beliefs.

Examples:

- market shift explanations
- founder/operator lessons
- teardown videos
- strategy breakdowns
- opinion-led category education

Best for:

- founder-led content
- category creation
- high-consideration SaaS
- narrative building

### Repurposable Interviews

Purpose: create a source asset for YouTube, LinkedIn, email, clips, and sales.

Examples:

- customer interviews
- operator interviews
- internal SME interviews
- webinar-style discussions
- podcast video episodes

Best for:

- small teams needing content leverage
- sales enablement
- community and partner-led distribution

## 3. Production Model

Start with repeatable formats instead of one-off creative ideas.

Recommended minimum operating model:

- 1 channel owner
- 1 subject-matter expert
- 1 repeatable script or outline format
- 1 publishing cadence
- 1 repurposing checklist
- 1 measurement dashboard

## 4. Distribution Workflow

Each video should become:

- YouTube upload
- LinkedIn post from the SME
- 3 to 5 short clips
- email/newsletter section
- sales follow-up asset
- blog or resource page embed when relevant
- internal enablement note for sales/customer success

## 5. Measurement

Do not use views as the only success metric.

Measure by content job:

- Tutorials: search impressions, watch time, assisted signups, support deflection
- POV videos: qualified engagement, comments, shares, newsletter growth, sales mentions
- Interviews: clip performance, partner distribution, pipeline influence, sales usage
- Product videos: demo page engagement, conversion assistance, onboarding usage

## Why This Source Matters

Vidyard is a B2B video platform, so its strategy guidance is directly relevant to SaaS teams building a serious YouTube program. This source is useful for turning "make videos" into a structured operating plan with goals, ownership, budget, video types, distribution, and measurement.

## Key Ideas

- Video strategy should begin with measurable business goals.
- B2B video does not need mass appeal; it should help the right buyers self-qualify.
- Video topics should be mapped to personas and buyer journey stages.
- Teams need to decide whether videos are campaign assets or evergreen program assets.
- Distribution channels should be planned before publishing.
- Ownership matters: scripting, approval, production, editing, and distribution need clear responsibilities.

## Useful Short Excerpt

> "The more specific, the better."

## Playbook Implications

- A B2B SaaS YouTube channel should define its audience tightly by ICP, role, pain, and journey stage.
- Measure different videos differently: tutorial videos, demo videos, thought-leadership videos, and campaign videos should not share one generic success metric.
- Establish a simple video operating model: owner, subject-matter expert, production format, publishing cadence, and distribution checklist.
- Avoid chasing broad reach if the better goal is qualified attention from a narrow buyer group.
